Social Engineering & Phishing

🎭

Social Engineering

Tricking people into doing what the attacker wants them to do.

🎪

Pretexting

Impersonate someone trusted — a boss, a friend, an official — and create a believable fake reason.

🎣

Phishing

The most common form — send a fake message that looks real, get the target to click, share, or obey.

15 / 28

Typical Components of a Phishing Email

1
Urgent Language
"ACCOUNT COMPROMISED: UPDATE PASSWORD NOW!"
2
Inconsistent Sender
alex@c0mpany.com — zero instead of "o"
3
Generic Greeting
"Dear User," — real companies know your name
4
Spelling Mistakes
"Update you're password" — typos and grammar errors
5
Shortened / Suspicious URLs
bitly.com/c0mpanyreset — hides the real destination from the victim

A Simple Hash (Ninjutsu)

  • Assign a number to each letter: A=1, B=2, C=3…
  • Add all numeric values in the message
  • Shadow Clan = 19+7+1+4+15+23+3+11+1+14 = 98
  • Divide by 100 — keep the last two digits
  • 98 ÷ 100 → remainder = 98 → hash is 98
